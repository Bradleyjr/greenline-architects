The Riverfront Residence began with a simple question from our clients: how do we live closer to the water without disturbing the landscape that drew us here?

Our response was a home that steps lightly on the land. Elevated on a series of slender concrete piers, the structure floats above the marsh grass, preserving the natural drainage patterns and root systems beneath. The building footprint was carefully negotiated with local ecologists to minimize disruption to the tidal ecosystem.

## Design Approach

The home is composed of three distinct volumes — a living pavilion, a sleeping wing, and a studio — connected by glass-enclosed walkways that frame views of the river at every turn. The material palette is deliberately restrained: board-formed concrete, blackened steel, and floor-to-ceiling glass. These hard materials are softened by the ever-changing light reflected off the water and the warm white oak used throughout the interiors.

## Sustainability

The residence operates almost entirely off-grid, with a 22kW solar array concealed within the roof planes and a geothermal exchange system that leverages the constant temperature of the river water. Rainwater is collected, filtered, and reused for irrigation and non-potable domestic needs. The landscape plan uses exclusively native species, requiring no irrigation once established.

## Recognition

The Riverfront Residence received a 2024 AIA Southeast Regional Design Award and was featured in Architectural Digest's "Best New Homes" issue.
