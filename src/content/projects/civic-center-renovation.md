When the City of Atlanta approached us about the civic center, the building had been largely abandoned for over a decade. Built in 1967, its raw concrete facades and monumental scale had fallen out of public favor. Our charge was not to erase the building's history but to unlock its potential as a gathering place for the next generation.

## Respecting the Original Structure

We began by stripping away decades of ad-hoc modifications to reveal the building's original structural logic — a series of massive concrete bents that create dramatic, column-free interior spans. These spaces, once subdivided into cramped offices, are now open and flooded with natural light through a series of carefully inserted skylights that follow the rhythm of the existing roof structure.

## Community-Centered Programming

The renovated center houses a public library branch, a community kitchen, co-working spaces for local nonprofits, a 400-seat flexible performance hall, and an outdoor amphitheater carved from the building's former loading dock. Every programmatic decision was informed by 18 months of community engagement sessions that reached over 3,000 Atlanta residents.

## Sustainable Renovation

Rather than demolish and rebuild, we chose to renovate — saving an estimated 12,000 tons of embodied carbon. New building systems achieve LEED Platinum certification, including a district-scale geothermal system, operable facade panels for natural ventilation, and a 500,000-gallon rainwater cistern beneath the new public plaza.

## Impact

Since reopening in early 2024, the civic center has hosted over 200 community events and attracted 150,000 visitors. It was named a finalist for the AIA National Honor Award for Architecture.
